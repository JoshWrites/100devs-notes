How to Network Deep Dive with Jason E. Torres
by Hamza
Key Takeaways:
Know Your Strengths
Understand and leverage your unique skills.,
Share your journey and projects on social media.,
Be responsive to messages.,
Aim to give more than you take.,
Engage in Conversations
Schedule intentional coffee chats.,
Value your and others’ time.,
For Introverts:
Focus on your “why” to help you step out of your comfort zone.,
Shared struggles can build connections.,
Join community spaces like Discord, Twitter Spaces, and tech forums.,
Building Connections:
Networking is a vital life skill.,
Engage genuinely with people’s projects and interests.,
Aim to build friendships and relationships rather than just job opportunities.,
Jason’s Insights:
Networking can significantly increase your chances of getting a job.,
Consistency and genuineness are crucial.,
Offer help and show up regularly without expecting immediate returns.,
Build connections organically rather than focusing on numbers.,
Quality Over Quantity:
Focus on meaningful connections rather than the number of connections.,
Regularly engage with a small group that supports and shows up for you.,
Tracking Network:
Jason doesn’t formally track his network to keep interactions genuine.,
General Advice:
Networking doesn’t have to be limited to tech people.,
Being nice and approachable is more important than being important.,
Take your time and be helpful to others.,
Quotes and Community Feedback:
“Give, give, give, ask.”
Helping others can indirectly help you solve your problems.,
Uncle Iroh: “Sometimes the best way to solve your own problems is to help someone else.” (edited)Friday, July 12, 2024 at 00:33

Practical Tips:
Post or show up consistently on social media and share your journey. {can just be replies, joining spaces},
Engage with familiar faces in community spaces.,
Use Twitter to share thoughts and connect with others.,
Be proactive in helping others, which will eventually pay off.,
Focus on building genuine relationships, not just increasing your follower count.,
Networking Strategy:
Start Small:
Identify a few key people who consistently show up for you.,
Invest time and effort in these relationships.,
Be Consistent:
Regularly engage with your network.,
Show genuine interest in their projects and successes.,
Stay Genuine:
Keep interactions sincere and not just for personal gain.,
Avoid rushing the process; let relationships grow organically.,
By following these guidelines, you’ll build a strong, genuine network that supports your growth and helps you succeed in your career. (edited)Friday, July 12, 2024 at 00:33
