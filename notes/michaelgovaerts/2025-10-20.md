● The goal is to get recommended so you have an easier time through the process ● Don’t make the take-home test your whole personality - you don’t want to overinvest, you wanna be in and out ● There’s always a potential reason to get rejected during a technical interview, and it’s just not worth the energy investment to only get rejected by the most niche reasons ● Whiteboard interviews aren’t as common, but recommend to practice a couple times just to be familiar with it ● Every time you practice interview or have an interview, always use the appropriate system to make sure you don’t walk in like an accident ● Try to look for the company's interview process when it’s a mid-size or higher company (smaller might be harder to find). You can find it through research or by simply asking. ● If you get blown off after asking what the interview will be like, ie. it’ll just be a technical, it’ll just be a behavior, make sure to ask for specifics and details with follow-up questions. Ie. cool, am I going to be expected to use x language? Should I be expected to walk through my code? Should I be expecting Leetcode hard/medium/easy questions? ● CAR - Cause Action Result ○ For all your behavioral questions and should be committed into memory and should have a lot of time invested into them. 3 sentences max for the beginning, but as you become more comfortable, you can add more if needed ○ There is no need to add in qualifiers, 100% all positive. There is no need to say anything negative to come off as more relatable. Think “why are you a genius” ○ Leon demonstrates asking a Banki question using CAR ○ You will talk more than you will memorize. The memorization is to give you a sense of comfort and make sure you cover all the important points. ● EUE - Explanation Use Example ○ Depending on the company you’re applying for, you might see yourself using this more ● Banki Questions ○ Will you be asked every question on this form? No. Will you be asked some questions that match exactly on the Banki form? Yes. The point is that if/when you are asked the same questions/questions in the same ballpark, you’ll be able to be more prepared to answer effectively ● PREP - parameters, returns, examples, pseudo code ● Leon uses PREP for Fizzbuzz and talks it out
