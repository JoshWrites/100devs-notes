What's the best way to find freelance work? Is cold calling the way?

Let everyone you know KNOW that you are a freelancer! All your friends, family members, church, community groups... Reach out to them and tell them what you're looking for. Make a landing page or a business card to show people what you can do. Just let everyone in your radius know what you can do! Some people are wlling to message everyone in their phone book and some people aren't. The people who are willing to are the ones who get their work, who get fantastic clients!

1) Let them know what you do, how you do it.

2) Being local is your SUPER POWER.

There are people who mass call, mass dial. Being local is a huge plus. You can walk into a building and say "I'm real. I'm here." Actually showing up, calling them, cold emailing, cold messaging, letting them know you are local and they can talk to you!!! Then spread out to other strategies using places where people are actually looking for work, but a lot of the platforms are not good experiences... but it's all individual perspectives.. See what works for you.

How can I manage my fleeting motivation during my 100-hour project and the trough of sorrows when I doubt my ability to succeed?

Figure out why your motivation/self-doubt are there... Address the root issues and then it should be easier. Don't go into your days like an accident. Have a game plan for them.
