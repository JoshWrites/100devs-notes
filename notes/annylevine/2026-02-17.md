Is it normal for 100Hours Project to take MORE than 100hours?

Yes, ABSOLUTELY. 100 Hours is the MINIMUM. Some people never stop working on theirs. Some people turn it into their business, or ... It becomes a lifelong thing.

It's just one component of what's going on to get a job.

Don't feel bad spending an hour on something small.

The point is that you're going to struggle SO MUCH that will help you LEARN.

Embrace the SUCK. Embrace the STRUGGLE. That's the POINT. You'll do fine.

Is it realistic to be a freelancer AND working a fulltime job?

Wait until you have at least 2 evergreen clients, and at least 3-6 months warchest, more if you have family/kids.

SO you have what you need to get through the stress.

Otherwise, going fulltime freelance is more stressful than it needs to be.

What to do when I get stuck on a task/project that I don't understand?

Make it fun to not understand something. It's a mindset thing. Some people are built different. If failure can be your fuel... If getting stuck on a coding problem doesn't mean danger to me, my loved ones, etc... everything else becomes a lot easier. Then you're doing puzzles or LEGO bricks. I have a deep-deated belief that given enough time and access to the internet, I can figure it out! Be bored. Once you make the binary decision, and let yourself be bored.. Eventually you can get your brain to view doing the thing you don't want to do as an actual reward.
