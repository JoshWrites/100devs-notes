\[2:32 PM Started\]

Topics:

ADHD and Engineering

Nothing inherent about SWE that prevents us from succeeding with personal limitations

If you have a limitation, it would likely impact you in any career. Use your resources to address those limitations, and then make sure to crush 100Devs and your amazing SWE career after

Lots of ADHD among the 100Devs community. Every person is different, though, so you need a plan that is tailored to you, and professionals can help with that

Don’t underestimate the power of lifestyle changes first, however. Medication does not need to be the first step, or even a necessary step.

\==== Break, as Michael was multitasking too hard ====

I think what was covered here was “Can we be a digital nomad in the current SWE market”?

Leon said yes. He thinks remote work is here to stay

Hardest part about remote work is that we often have to be in the correct time zone

(From Michael): However, some companies probably want coverage at odd hours (like graveyard or overnight shifts). Thus, if you’re in a distant time zone, these might be normal daytime working hours for you. This could be extra great since, in many places, graveyard/overnight hours are +not+ popular, and sometimes they’re paid more highly as a result. (This is usually the case in healthcare, in my experience, for example.)

However, Leon has seen some companies (like Railway) go fully remote, global, and fully asynchronous

Employees just commit to working at a specific time period, for a standard full-time commitment, and it doesn’t matter when they’re working. They just need to show the company and their team that they’re dependable

Leon has also found StarLink to be very helpful to allow him to be more remote with his current work. He has done some 100Devs Huddles outdoors using StarLink to get connected, and he doesn’t think we have noticed.

\===

Topic: AI is increasing the pace of development, but most of the work of an SWE is not coding

Leon does not see the human in the SWE loop going anywhere anytime soon

Need to know the fundamentals to make sure you know that the AI is doing the right stuff, and helping you build the product that your stakeholders need

Danny’s Masterclass on this is really good, though. Helps with AI prompting

\====

How much notice do you need to give part-time jobs?

Rarely need to give notice at all, but part-time jobs especially. They’ll fire you without a thought (or just stop giving you hours)

Don’t hesitate to pursue better opportunities, but you can always feel free to give the current position an opportunity to keep you there

Two weeks is industry-standard for courtesy

Leon only has loyalty to people, like those on his team who he wants to keep in his network as good contacts. He has no loyalty to the company itself

\===

\[2:46 PM\]

Back to what happens if AI makes perfect code?

Lots of opportunities for SWE, especially once everyone realizes that SWEs are necessary, since coding is not all that’s necessary

Leon is not worried at all. If companies can move faster, then they need more people

IBM just says they plan to increase new hires in 2026 by six times what they were doing before

AI can get new people productive day one, so now they need as many bodies as possible to take advantage of that. No need to onboard or onramp, or at least much less of a need

Lots of companies also don’t want to give up secrets to AI

2:48 PM PT

Remote in IT but want to be an SWE

Boss took a chance on me. Should I stay?

Leon thinks this is unlikely

If certs at job lead to raises? Then go ahead. “Taking a chance on me” isnt meaningful. If they want to pay you, great. If not, go somewhere that will give you better pay and career stability, and help you pursue what you really want to do (in this case, being an SWE)

Leon keeping up to date with software

Need fundamentals and theory. This helps you know what the AI is working off of, and what it’s doing

Need enough theory to not be behind, but need to be up to date (HackerNews, Twitter)

Backfilling theory, getting his edge by being current, and then building repeatable systems that have high-output when he’s using AI

Leon has no problem being confident, because he works hard to justify it

\[3:06 PM PT\]

New Dev Routine?

Same thing as always: Don’t make each day an accident

Every day: Learning, Review, Building, and Networking

Learning: 100Devs, Books, Videos, etc.

Review: Anki, something that helps actively recall

Building: Use the things you just learned

Networking:

If you keep doing these things consistently, you get an opportunity. That’s how it works. Focus on what works

\===

\[3:07 PM PT\]

Leon does not price with freelancing

Finds out what client’s budget is, and then he finds out what he can do for that price point

Pricing in advance will cause a client to ask for everything but expect the price you set

Price point just makes customers unhappy. Does not help you at all

Understand customer first, then determine if their budget will allow you to help them based on how much it will cost to make it make sense to you

Leah apparently said in chat she’s having trouble with too much work using this method. (Leon says it’s not a problem for him either)

Using this strategy works for both of them

\[3:09 PM PT\]

Work does not come from your job, unless you’re +VERY+ lucky (Laravel seems awesome)

Growth therefore has to come from outside of work

Too tired at the end of day? Have to get up early and study before work.

Never eat lunch alone

Keep stability of the current role until you get a new one

This is how you transition to SWE and succeed at doing so

Leon’s normal schedule:

Teaching a little later (was 7AM before, now it’s 8AM)

9AM-11AM is coding for Leon (7-8 PM is also coding, if he can)

Before 9 AM is family time

Working remotely? How to eat lunch alone?

Virtual lunch works. Do a Zoom eating lunch together. Book that time

Very rare. You do open source because it’s fun to do and it helps you provide proof

Does not help you connect with a job. You have to go out there and Hunt

Some open source projects do hire, but it’s very rare and unlikely to be as successful as Hunting according to the 100Devs advice

Need more than certs to set yourself apart

System design course or tutorial?

\===

Only front-end dev on backend team. What to do?

Growth does not come from job. Advice is to find the growth, mentorship, and help in other places so you feel like you’re growing

Also check with the team and management. “I love the work I do, I love my team. I don’t feel like I’m growing.”

Maybe they’ll pay for memberships and conferences

Any leader worth their salt wants their people to feel like their growing

This is a great conversation to have. Leon loves if people who report to him ask him for help to be happier, healthier, or feel more like they’re growing. As their manager, this is Leon’s job

Could be snitching on yourself if you don’t frame it right. “I love everything about what I’m doing, but I feel like I am missing opportunities to grow. (Leadership growth, etc.)”

Probably not a good conversation if you don’t have a good relationship with management, but you need to work on these relationships, too

Don’t want to let people think you’re looking to quit. Need to emphasize how happy you are otherwise

\===

Last Round of Interview. They loved me. Now they’re saying there’s a delay. Do I wait?

This happens. This is why you need to have multiple opportunities lined up

They are likely lying to you. Telling you and ten other candidates that they want to move on with you

May end up hiring someone internally, and the eleven of you are out of luck

This is why you have to always keep looking for opportunities. If you slow down whenever stuff starts to feel good, you’re now in big trouble with your lack of momentum if those pan out. (They often DON’T pan out.)

Don’t ride the rollercoaster, just keep getting those interviews lined up through HitListing, Banki, Leetcode, etc.

\[3:26 PM PT\]

If growth isn’t at job, why are years of experience important?

Years of experience has nothing to do with working

Years of doing engineering. That may be on the job or outside of the job

It’s also +ALL MADE UP+

No way to easily quantify how good of an engineer you are. “10 years of experience” makes this easy

Kids coming out of Waterloo can run circles around “senior” engineer in Midwest

Proxy that filters out most people, but it’s not anything worth paying attention to

See tips above to make sure you’re always growing

Front-End developer at day job? You’re atrophying elsewhere if you’re not continuing to work on them, especially outside of the job

Most people aren’t passionate about this stuff, so you have to actively work at it, otherwise you’re going to end up in a place career-wise that you don’t want to be

\[3:28 PM PT\]

Commission-based Software Engineering role at a Startup? Pay is based on profits?

This is awful. Do not go through with it.

This is a scam.

Whenever someone asks this question, someone in chat usually says something

“Hey Leon. I got into Y Combinator. Getting guaranteed C funding. Can I give you equity beforehand? I can show you proof of all of this”

This works. But mostly, it’s just people with an idea trying to get developers to work for free. Don’t fall for it
