by choir241 (Richard)
10-21-2025
● Funnel where we’re eventually going to put opportunities into this funnel
○ The most important part of the funnel is the top - there’s no point optimizing the
end parts like data structures and algorithms if you can’t even get the interview
● Workshopping Banki questions and answers live
Volunteer 1:
Banki 1. Give me an example of the project or initiative that you started on your own. It can be a
non-business one. What prompted you to get started?
● A bit long, got a bit lost. Needs to focus on using a strict CAR response
● Leon Q: How much time do you think that saved?
● Leon Q: How many people are using the solution you made?
● Leon Q: How many people were doing this before you?
● We’re here to talk about ourselves in the best light possible - peel off all the layers and
present the gold
● Record yourself and hear yourself as you practice banki questions
● In one sentence, what was the problem?
● In one sentence, what was the solution?
● Read more technical content to become more familiar and comfortable with terminology
Volunteer 2:
Banki 4. Tell me about when you had to deal with conflict within your team. How was the conflict
solved? How did you handle that? How would you deal with it now?
● Missing result from CAR formula
● Painting yourself being a little too harsh during the conflict (in the way you paint yourself)
may cause the interviewer to focus on that for the rest of the interview
● Don’t use any technical jargon words or acronyms - there’s a really good chance your
interviewer doesn’t know them
● It’s best to think things through before you get to the actual interview so that you’re not
caught off guard
● Impact needs to feel more impactful
● These kinds of questions are trick questions to have you snitch on yourself
Volunteer 3
9. Tell me about a situation when you were responsible for project planning. Did everything go
according to your plan? If not, then why and what kind of counteractions did you have to take?
● Waited until the very end for the actual banger
● What you consider a small win might actually be a huge win
● Don’t initially give all the details regarding background information - give initial tight CAR
answer first, and then IF they ask for more details, that’s where you give them that info
○ This way you have more of a back and forth conversation instead of rambling on
for too long and keeping it focused on what you want to cover
● It’s important to know what the correct labels are and organizing the story so it’s easy
and digestible to understand
Q&A
Q: Should I spin my non-tech background in a way that sounds like it’s more of a technical
background?
A: Yes, they’re hiring for a software engineer, so your experience should sound like you’re a
software engineer and have technical background as much as possible
Q: How should I list my 100Hrs project in my resume?
A: Leon lists it under experience and labels the project as an open source project. Especially
since this is a project that had 100+ hrs put into it.
Q: I have a one year gap (or longer) on my resume/experience, how do I list it on my resume?
A: You were freelancing (or really freelancing). You don’t want to make them guess and it makes
sense.
